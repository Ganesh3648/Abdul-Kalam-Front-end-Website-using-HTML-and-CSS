More info about A.P.J.Abdul Kalam

  1. Early Life and Education:
    • Kalam was born into a humble Tamil Muslim family. His father, Jainulabudeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife.
    • He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and went on to study Aerospace Engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
  2. Career at ISRO and DRDO:
    • He joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1962, where he contributed significantly to India's space program.
    • Later, he worked at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and played a crucial role in developing India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV) and ballistic missile systems.
  3. Missile Program:
    • Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of several key projects, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles, which bolstered India's strategic capabilities.
  4. Presidency:
    • He was elected as the 11th President of India in 2002 and served until 2007. He was widely regarded as the "People's President" due to his affable and accessible nature.
  5. Philosophy and Vision:
    • Dr. Kalam was an ardent advocate for education and believed that it was the key to a nation's progress. He emphasized the need for India to become a developed nation by 2020, which he referred to as Vision 2020.
  6. Authorship:
    • He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiographical account of his early life and work in the space and missile programs, which became immensely popular.
  7. Legacy:
    • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam is widely respected for his contributions to science, technology, and education in India. He is remembered for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to the country.
  8. Awards and Honors:
    • He received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997.
  9. Death:
    • Dr. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He suffered a cardiac arrest.
  10. Inspirational Figure:
    • Even after his passing, Dr. Abdul Kalam continues to inspire people, especially the youth, through his writings, speeches, and his exemplary life.

Dr. Abdul Kalam's contributions to science, technology, and his unwavering dedication to the progress of India have left an indelible mark on the nation's history. He is remembered as one of India's most beloved and respected figures.
